When using computers remember:

SUPREME

S

ecurity over accessibility

It's important to balance how easy your account is to log into, and how secure it should be. Measures like Multi Factor Authentication (sending a security code to your email or phone), can be annoying at times but will save your account when it's needed. These few extra seconds to log in will save you weeks to months of headache in the future when your account is breached.

U

ser session

When logged into a computer that is used by other people, it is extremely important to log out of any accounts every time you leave. This is to ensure no one has access to your accounts without your knowledge or permission. Even if it is your personal computer, it is wise to log out every time you are leaving the physical area. 

P

olicy of least privilege

When dealing with many accounts for an organization or company, you should only give accounts the amount of permission they need to accomplish their job. If you give everyone full administrative access, it creates a vulnerability where losing anyones account can be fatal to your company.

R

eusing passwords

You should NEVER reuse a password from any other account. Data breaches are very frequent, and once a single password associated with your email gets leaked. Malicious hackers can use that to try and log into other accounts. So while some accounts might seem insignificant, you should always use a unique password. Password managers help a lot, and there are a lot of options online for various applications.

Also remember to use a password of at least 10-12 characters. For reference, an 8 character password that includes lowercase, uppercase, numbers, and special characters takes (roughly) 12 minutes to brute force. A 12 character one with the same criteria, takes 20 years. Prioritize length over complexity, but it does not hurt to have both.

E

xternal media

As in USB sticks, or various plug in storage devices. You should not plug in anything that you do not know where it came from or whose it is. Especially on work or computers that are not yours. It's not uncommon for hackers to drop USB sticks with malicious files outside of corporate buildings to try and get access to the computers outside.

M

alicious sites and files

Websites can be disguised as anything, the same goes for files. While you might think you're installing a new updated version of google chrome, you could be installing ransomware that encrypts your computer and demands money. Make sure to use caution when dealing with any website, and don't blindly accept downloads unless you are actively expecting one and trust it.

E

mails

Phishing is extremely prevalent today and is the most common type of cyberattack. The dangers of phishing are how easy it is, and how it can be disguised as anything. Take this entire project as an example, I was able to get you to click on this link. Discretely installing a file, or making my website look like a Google log in, isn't hard and is a common way to steal passwords. Another huge risk is links. Links can be sending you anywhere, it is often smart to open a new tab and navigate to where ever you need from there and not follow any suspicious links.
